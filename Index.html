About King Ramses II

King Ramses II is one of the most important and greatest kings of ancient Egyptian civilization. He lived for 90 years and ruled Egypt for 68 years. King Ramses II surpassed any other Egyptian king in leaving numerous monuments across Egypt, from north to south. Among his most significant architectural achievements are the Abu Simbel Temple in Aswan and a statue of him in the Mit Rahina Museum in Giza. There are also monuments bearing his name outside Egypt, such as the obelisk currently located in the Place de la Concorde in Paris, which was moved from its original site in front of the Luxor Temple.

The Story of the Grand Egyptian Museum Egypt’s Gift to the World

In 1992, Egypt unveiled plans for a monumental project worthy of its ancient heritage: the Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM). Designed to be the world’s largest museum dedicated to a single civilization, the GEM embodies a visionary mission—to safeguard Egypt’s timeless legacy while presenting it through a modern lens. This architectural marvel was conceived to transform Egypt into a global cultural landmark, honoring its revered title as the 'Mother of the World'.
