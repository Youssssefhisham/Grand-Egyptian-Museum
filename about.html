About the Grand Egyptian Museum

In 1992, Egypt announced a monumental project worthy of its ancient heritage: the Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM), the largest museum in the world dedicated to a single civilization. The vision was to preserve Egypt’s timeless legacy and present it in a modern way, transforming it into a global cultural landmark befitting the "Mother of the World."

The Beginning and Planning

Location: Chosen near the Giza Plateau, just two kilometers from the Pyramids, seamlessly linking the grandeur of the past with the innovation of the future.

Design: Won by an Irish firm in an international competition, the museum’s architecture harmonizes with the Pyramids while incorporating cutting-edge exhibition technologies.

Challenges and Achievements

Relocating Artifacts: The project involved the largest artifact transfer in history, including the Khufu Solar Boat and the colossal statues of Ramses II, using precision techniques to ensure their preservation.

Delays: Despite financial and logistical hurdles, the GEM became a symbol of Egyptian perseverance, with contributions from experts across 20 countries.

Treasures of the Museum

  • The complete treasures of Tut ankhamun (more than 5,000 items) displayed together for the first time.
  • The Hall of Ramses II, featuring his 11-meter-tall statue.
  • An interactive visitor journey tracing Egypt’s history from prehistoric times to the Greco-Roman era.

Opening and Vision

After years of anticipation, the museum began welcoming visitors in phases starting in 2023, with gradual gallery openings. It is poised to become the world’s premier tourist destination, attracting millions annually and sending a timeless message: "Civilization is unforgettable, and heritage is eternal."

"This museum is not just for Egypt—it is a gift to all humanity." — Statement by Egypt’s Minister of Antiquities

Mission:

The GEM is dedicated to developing a sustainable model as a world-class iconic destination by displaying unique and significant exhibits, promoting Egyptian heritage locally and internationally, integrating cultural and entertainment components, creating an inspiring visitor experience, fostering research and innovation in the field of heritage management, and applying the highest management and operation standards and technologies with the goal of contributing to national economic development.

Vision:

The GEM will be the largest and most visited museum in the world belonging to a single civilisation and contribute to the unique positioning of Egypt as a premier destination and global cultural hub.

Journey

The story of the Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM) is a success story. The iconic Egyptian Museum, Cairo first opened its doors in 1902 but after over 90 years of service, it no longer could meet the needs of its ever-expanding collections and visitorship. International tourism and local interest in history and heritage were ever growing, and Egypt needed to innovate.

Plans to build a large, modern museum were first announced in 1992, and through careful development, the Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM) Project began to form. By 2002 the foundation stone of GEM had been laid at a site overlooking the legendary Giza Pyramids and an international architectural competition was underway to design the new museum. Held under the auspices of UNESCO and the International Federation of Architects, the winning design by unanimous decision belonged to Róisín Heneghan and Shi-Fu Peng (Heneghan Peng Architects, Ireland).

Construction of the museum complex began in 2005, but a series of environmental, financial, and political setbacks soon created a series of delays for the ambitious project. Despite adversity, the Grand Egyptian Museum Conservation Centre (GEM CC) opened in 2010 and is the largest conservation centre in the Middle East. Thanks to the dedicated and focused work of all involved, the following years brought GEM ever closer to completion. With thoughts turned towards the health and safety of all during the Covid-19 Global Pandemic, GEM delayed its public opening until 2021. When open, the GEM will welcome the world to explore its unique cultural landscape – an innovative and technologically integrated museum and recreational centre.

About The Operator

A subsidiary of Hassan Allam Holding, Legacy for Management and Development, specializes in the management and operation of premium destinations. Hassan Allam Holding is one of the largest privately owned corporations in Egypt and the MENA region, with over 85 years of experience in engineering, construction, building materials, and utilities, with over 45,000 employees.

In March 2021, Legacy for Management and Development won the contract to provide and operate services and facilities at the Grand Egyptian Museum for 12 years. As the operator of the GEM project, Legacy adopted a new business model for museum management in Egypt to support the sustainable growth of the project. Legacy aims to integrate cultural, commercial, and entertainment experiences at the GEM to create an inspiring visitor destination and enhance Egypt's position as a world-class tourist destination, contributing significantly to Egypt's economic development.
